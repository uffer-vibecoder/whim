# Design prompt for Claude — Cartwheel

Paste the block below into Claude (web/desktop, with visual artifacts on). Trim the
"Screens to design" list to whatever you want in one pass — asking for 2–3 screens at a
time gives sharper results than all of them at once.

---

You are a senior product & brand designer. Design the visual identity and key screens for
**Cartwheel**, an "American dopamine store."

**The concept.** Cartwheel is a *fake* e-commerce store. People browse real-looking
products, add to cart, "check out" for $0, and watch a live delivery truck drive to their
home — but nothing is ever charged and nothing ever ships. It delivers the *thrill* of
shopping (the anticipation is most of the reward) with none of the cost or regret. Tagline:
**"All the shopping, none of the spending."**

**The emotional brief — this is the most important part.** Cartwheel exists *because*
impulse spending can be harmful. So the design must hold a tension: **playful and
dopamine-rich, but never predatory.** It should feel like a warm, slightly silly toy that's
in on the joke — celebratory confetti energy, not casino-slot manipulation. Think "joyful
parody of a shopping app," not "growth-hacked checkout funnel." Avoid urgency dark patterns
(fake countdowns to pressure, guilt, "you're missing out"). Celebrate *browsing* and *not
buying*; a running "money you saved by not buying" tally is a hero element, not a footnote.

**Current art direction (extend or improve it):**
- Fonts: Baloo 2 (display, rounded, friendly) + Nunito (body).
- Palette: candy gradients — pink #ff5da2, purple #7c5cff, teal #3dd6c4, yellow #ffd23f,
  orange #ff8a5c; warm off-white paper #fff6fb; ink #2b1738.
- Soft 24px radii, pillowy shadows, gradient "product tiles," lots of rounded pills.
- Currently product images are emoji on gradient tiles — we want to evolve toward real /
  AI product photography while keeping the candy-colored, playful frame.

**Audience & platform.** Gen Z / millennials who shop online for fun and stress relief;
mobile-first, also great on desktop. Responsive.

**Deliverables:**
1. A tightened **brand system** — logo concept for "Cartwheel" 🛒, color tokens, type scale,
   button/pill/card styles, iconography style, and a short "voice & tone" note (witty, warm,
   self-aware; never shaming).
2. High-fidelity **screen mockups** (see list) with real-feeling content.
3. A signature **"delivery tracking" moment** — make this the dopamine payoff: a cute
   top-down map, an animated truck, a satisfying ETA + step tracker, and a delightful
   "delivered (but nothing actually came)" reveal.
4. One **empty/edge state** done with personality (empty cart).
5. Notes on **motion**: where micro-animations, confetti, and sound cues should fire — and
   where restraint matters (respect prefers-reduced-motion).

**Screens to design:**
- Storefront / home (hero, category chips, product grid, one small clearly-labeled "Sponsored" slot)
- Product detail (photo, playful fake reviews, price, add-to-cart)
- Cart + checkout (fake payment, a "delivery window" picker: choose 30s–3h or "Surprise me",
  and a big "$0.00 actually charged" reassurance)
- Live delivery tracking (the hero moment)
- A "Drops" screen — limited new-arrival releases with a countdown and a friendly "notify me"

**Constraints / must-haves:**
- Always make it obvious this is a parody: "Sponsored" labels, "$0 charged," "nothing ships."
- Keep the one ad slot tasteful, contextual, and visually distinct from products (never disguised).
- Accessibility: legible contrast, large tap targets, reduced-motion friendly.

Show the brand system first, then the screens. For each screen, include a one-line note on
the intended *feeling* and the key interaction. Feel free to propose a sharper logo, a mascot,
or a more refined palette if you can improve on the current direction — explain your choices.
